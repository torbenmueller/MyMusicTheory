Sentence Form

When composing a melody, it is helpful to use the sentence form as a guide. This creates self-contained musical sections and the themes and motifs can be easily understood and memorized by the listener.

Well suited for: Main Themes B Themes Intros Outros Transitions Adventure Theme Opening Allegro to a Sonata
Characteristics: Energy Action Motion

Ludwig van Beethoven - Sonata No. 1 in F minor, Op. 2 No. 1

Start with a 2 bar basic idea. Repeat the basic idea with or without variation. Bars 1-4 are called the presentation. It should have a feeling of harmonic stability. The continuation in bar 5-6 is where the magic happens. It should have a feeling of forward motion and movement. This could mean more motivic units in less time, more chords per bar or more active rhythm. It moves us from the bginning to the end. In this example it moves us to the highest note of the theme, the C5 at the beginning of bar 7. The cadence finally closes off the theme. It could be a perfect cadence or a half cadence. But there should be a sence of punctuation, a breath, where the listener can rest.
